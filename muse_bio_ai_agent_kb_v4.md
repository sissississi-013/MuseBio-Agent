**MUSE BIO IS NOT A MEDICAL PRACTICE. WE CANNOT AND DO NOT PROVIDE MEDICAL ADVICE, DIAGNOSIS, OR TREATMENT.**

### What the AI Agent MUST NEVER DO:

- ❌ Diagnose any medical condition
- ❌ Interpret test results or symptoms
- ❌ Recommend treatments or medications
- ❌ Tell someone whether they should see a doctor for a specific symptom
- ❌ Provide medical advice about conditions, medications, or health decisions
- ❌ Say "you probably have X condition" or "that sounds like Y"
- ❌ Suggest stopping or starting any medication
- ❌ Provide guidance on managing medical conditions
- ❌ Say "we provide care" - WE ARE NOT A MEDICAL PRACTICE

### Why:
- Muse Bio is NOT a medical practice
- Our team does NOT have the training, licensing, or legal authority to practice medicine
- We are a research and commercial donation program, NOT a healthcare provider
- Providing medical advice without proper credentials is illegal and dangerous

### ALWAYS Redirect to Healthcare Providers:

**Approved responses for medical questions:**
- "That's a great question for your healthcare provider! We're not a medical practice and can't provide medical advice."
- "We'd need to discuss your specific health situation during your health screening interview to determine eligibility, but we can't diagnose or treat conditions."
- "For questions about your health, symptoms, or medications, please consult with your healthcare provider."
- "We can tell you about our eligibility criteria, but only your healthcare provider can advise you about your specific health situation."

### What We CAN Do:
- ✅ Explain our eligibility criteria in general terms
- ✅ Say certain conditions are "assessed during health screening interview"
- ✅ Provide information about our testing panel (what we test for)
- ✅ Explain what happens if test results are positive (we provide referrals and a letter)
- ✅ Share general information about menstrual cups and how to use them
- ✅ Describe our programs and procedures

### Red Flag Examples:

**❌ BAD:** "That spotting you're describing could be endometriosis."  
**✅ GOOD:** "For concerns about your menstrual symptoms, please consult your healthcare provider. During our health screening interview, we can discuss whether any diagnosed conditions affect eligibility."

## 4. Commercial Donation Program

### Program Details
- **Type:** Commercial donation (not a research study)
- **Conflict of Interest:** Staff running this program have ownership in Muse Bio and benefit financially when products are sold. We're being transparent so you can make an informed decision.

### Sample Processing Journey
1. **Cell Isolation** - Separate stem cells from menstrual blood
2. **Cell Culture** - Grow cells in special media in the lab
3. **Secretion Collection** - Cells produce proteins, growth factors, beneficial substances - we collect these
4. **Cell Removal** - Completely filter out ALL cells, leaving just beneficial secretions
5. **Commercial Sale** - Sell cell-free ingredient to cosmetic manufacturers

**About the Products:**
- We're a **supplier** - we provide ingredients, not final products
- Nothing on market yet - building toward it
- Cosmetic products only - for appearance enhancement
- No health claims, no therapeutic uses

### Ownership & Rights (CRITICAL)

**What You Give Up AFTER PROCESSING BEGINS:**
- ❌ All ownership rights - cells aren't yours anymore
- ❌ All commercial rights - Muse Bio owns everything: cells, ingredient, products
- ❌ Control over use - we can sell worldwide, make unlimited profit, license to others
- ❌ Future money - just one-time $150, no royalties ever, no matter how successful products become

**Legal Basis:** Based on Moore v. Regents of the University of California (1990) - once biological material is processed, you don't own it anymore.

**What You Keep:**
- ✅ Right to say no to future donations
- ✅ Right to get copy of consent form
- ✅ Right to ask questions anytime
- ✅ Right to request deletion of personal info (de-identified data stays)

**What You CAN'T Do AFTER PROCESSING:**
- ❌ Can't take back your donation
- ❌ Can't stop products from being made or sold
- ❌ Can't get any additional money ever
- ❌ Can't find out which specific products have your donation

### Can You Change Your Mind?

**Before processing begins:** Yes! Change your mind anytime, no problem.

**After processing starts (within hours of collection):** No, sorry. Once biological processes start, they can't be reversed. That's why we make sure you really understand this before you donate!

You can always stop future donations, but past ones are permanent after processing begins.

**Per Donation:**

**IMPORTANT - Payment Timing:**
- Goodie bag: Immediately after donation

## 6. Complete Menstrual Cup Guide

### What Is a Menstrual Cup?
A small, flexible cup made of medical-grade silicone that sits in your vaginal canal (like where a tampon goes, but lower). It collects menstrual blood instead of absorbing it.

### Why They're Awesome

**For the Planet:**
- Average person uses 10,000-15,000 tampons/pads in lifetime (250-300 lbs waste!)
- One cup lasts 10 years
- Reduces carbon footprint by 16x
- 99% lower environmental impact

**For Your Body:**
- No bleaches, fragrances, or weird fibers
- No Toxic Shock Syndrome risk
- Doesn't dry you out or mess with pH
- Way more comfortable once you get used to it

**For Your Wallet:**
- Save $60-120/year
- Over lifetime: $2,000-$3,000 saved!

**For Your Life:**
- Wear up to 12 hours (vs 4-8 for tampons)
- Holds 3x more than super tampon
- Swimming, yoga, sleeping - do everything
- No more emergency store runs

### Before You Start: Optional Cervix Check

Not required, but can be helpful!

Wash hands, then insert index or middle finger into vagina. Your cervix feels like the tip of your nose but with texture like inside of your cheek.

- **Low cervix:** Feel it at first knuckle → might want shorter cup
- **High cervix:** Need whole finger or can't reach it → cup will sit higher

### Step-by-Step: Using Your Cup

#### STEP 1: CLEAN IT

**First Time (or Between Periods):**
1. Boil cup for 5-7 minutes (not longer!)
2. Use tongs or put in whisk so it doesn't touch pot bottom
3. Let it cool completely

**Before Inserting:**
1. Wash hands really well
2. Rinse cup with clean water
3. Optional: Use tiny bit of water-based lube if you want

#### STEP 2: FOLD IT

**Try these three methods:**

**The C-Fold (Best for Beginners):**
- Flatten the cup
- Fold it in half to make "C" shape
- Pinch firmly below rim to keep folded

**The Punch-Down:**
- Push rim down into base with your finger
- Makes narrow point at top
- Good if you have low cervix

**The 7-Fold:**
- Flatten the cup
- Take right corner and fold across to make "7" shape
- Creates tapered point

**Pro tip:** Practice folding before your period starts!

#### STEP 3: INSERT IT

1. **Get comfy** - Sit on toilet, squat, or stand with one leg up
2. **Optional:** Use warm water or water-based lube to make easier
3. **Position hands:**
   - One hand: Hold folded cup firmly
   - Other hand: Use fingers to gently spread labia
4. **Insert slowly:**
   - Aim toward tailbone (not straight up!)
   - Slide in at slight angle
   - Once rim is in, shimmy fingers down as you push cup further
5. **Let it go** - Cup will pop open

#### STEP 4: CHECK THE SEAL

The seal prevents leaks!

- Insert one finger alongside cup
- Run it around rim - should feel smooth and round, not folded
- Give base gentle tug - should feel resistance (that's the suction!)
- Can also rotate cup side to side to ensure it opened fully

**Pro tip:** Bear down with pelvic floor muscles to create space for cup to open.

When sealed right, you should NOT feel the cup at all. If you feel it, might not be in right spot.

#### STEP 5: WEAR TIME

- Can wear 4-8 hours depending on flow
- Never more than 12 hours!
- Day 2 usually has heaviest flow

**Signs It's Full:**
- Bubbling sensation
- Light leakage
- Feeling of heaviness
- Cup sitting lower than usual
- Mild pressure

**For Muse Bio:** Once you have at least 5mL (Day 2 usually gets there in 4-8 hours), head to our office at your scheduled time!

#### STEP 6: REMOVAL

**For Muse Bio Collection:** You'll do this in our private bathroom at Frontier Tower! Carefully remove cup and pour into vial using funnel.

**For Regular Home Use:**
1. Wash hands
2. Get in comfy position
3. Insert fingers and find base of cup
4. Pinch base to break seal (might hear little "pop")
5. Slowly pull out while keeping upright
6. Empty into toilet
7. Rinse and reinsert (or wash and store)

### Common Questions

**Q: Can I pee with it in?**  
A: Yep! Urethra (pee hole) and vagina are different openings. Cup doesn't interfere.

**Q: Can I poop with it in?**  
A: Technically yes, but some find it shifts when bearing down. If uncomfortable, just remove and reinsert.

**Q: Which size do I need?**  
A: Generally: under 30 and no vaginal births = smaller size; over 30 or have given birth = larger size. But cervix height and flow matter too! We'll help you figure it out.

**Q: Will I feel it?**  
A: Nope! Once it's in right, you shouldn't feel a thing.

**Q: Can it get lost?**  
A: No way! Vagina is closed tube. Cervix is at the end, and cup can't go past it. You can always get it out.

**Q: What if I can't get it out?**  
A: Stay calm! Bear down with pelvic floor (like you're pooping), and it'll come lower. Pinch base to break seal, then pull. If really stuck, contact us or your healthcare provider.

**Q: Is it messy?**  
A: Can be at first while learning! But most people get the hang of it quickly. Practice at home before your first donation.

### Cleaning Your Cup

- **During period:** Rinse with water between uses
- **Between periods:** Boil for 5-7 minutes
- **Storage:** Keep in clean, breathable bag
- **Never:** Use harsh chemicals, bleach, or dishwasher!

## 8. Eligibility Requirements

### Basic Requirements

You're potentially eligible if you:
- ✅ Are **between 18-45 years old**
- ✅ **Menstruate regularly**
- ✅ Are in **general good health**
- ✅ Can **get to San Francisco** (Frontier Tower)

### Health Questionnaire & Screening

Both programs require health questionnaire. For commercial program, you'll fill it out independently, then review with staff in health screening interview.

---

### CANNOT DONATE (Permanent or Indefinite Deferral)

#### Pregnancy & Reproductive:
- Currently pregnant
- Breastfeeding or within 6 months postpartum (must be >6 months AND have had 1-2 normal cycles)
- Amenorrhea (no period) for >6 months
- **Currently have an IUD** (must be removed + 90 day wait period)
- **Diagnosed endometriosis** (with formal diagnosis OR pathology/imaging confirmation)
- History of genital herpes (HSV-1 or HSV-2) - any history
- Endometrial ablation, hysterectomy, or D&C

#### Infectious Diseases:
- HIV positive (HIV-1 or HIV-2)
- Hepatitis B positive
- Hepatitis C positive
- HTLV positive
- CMV positive (Cytomegalovirus)
- Chagas disease
- Malaria or dengue
- Babesiosis
- Any history of IV drug use (non-prescription)

#### Cancers:
- Cervical, ovarian, or uterine cancer
- Sickle cell disease (note: sickle cell TRAIT is okay!)
- Blood cancers, leukemia, lymphoma, myeloproliferative disorders

#### Other Serious Conditions:
- Organ or tissue transplant recipient
- Ever taken immunosuppressant medications
- Chronic anticoagulation therapy
- CJD/TSE or family member with CJD/TSE
- Lived in UK 3+ months between 1980-1996
- Lived in Europe 5+ years between 1980-2001
- Received blood, transplants, or human-derived products in UK/France

---

### TEMPORARY DEFERRAL (Must Wait Before Donating)

#### Recent Procedures/Treatments:
- IUD insertion or removal: **90 days**
- Gynecologic procedures: **90 days**
- Major surgery: **90 days** after full recovery
- Recent hospitalization: **90 days** after discharge
- Recent antibiotic use for infection: **14 days** after last dose
- Recent systemic corticosteroids: **30 days** after last dose
- Recent tattoo/piercing (unlicensed facility): **12 months**
- Topical vaginal medications: **72 hours**

#### Recent Infections/Illnesses:
- Active symptoms (fever, cough, etc.): Until symptom-free + appropriate period
- Recent STI diagnosis and treatment:
  - Chlamydia/Gonorrhea: **21 days** after treatment + negative test of cure
  - Trichomoniasis: **21 days** after treatment
  - Bacterial vaginosis: **21 days** after treatment
  - Active genital warts: Until treated + healed + **30 days**
  - Syphilis: Must have documented adequate treatment

#### Risk Factors:
- Sexual contact with high-risk individuals: **12 months**
- Sex for money/drugs: **12 months**
- Blood transfusion: **12 months**
- Needlestick injury: **12 months**
- Incarcerated >72 hours: **12 months** from release
- Babesia-endemic state outdoor exposure: **6 months**

---

### CONDITIONALLY ELIGIBLE (Assessed Case-by-Case)

These conditions might be okay depending on severity and other factors:
- PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) - as long as cycles are regular enough
- Uterine fibroids - as long as not causing severe irregularity
- Irregular cycles - depends on pattern and consistency
- Past medical history of controlled conditions (thyroid, diabetes, etc.)
- Tobacco or alcohol use
- Birth control use (pills, patch, ring - all generally fine!)
- Tubal ligation
- Resolved medical conditions from >12 months ago

**Note:** Just because something is on this list doesn't guarantee eligibility - we'll discuss your specific situation during health screening interview!

- **❌ IUDs (both hormonal and copper):** NOT eligible
  - Must be removed + 90 day wait period

#### Endometriosis:
- **❌ Formal diagnosis by doctor:** NOT eligible
- **✅ Suspicion without confirmed diagnosis:** May be eligible (case-by-case)

#### Cervical Issues:
- **✅ Past cervical dysplasia (LSIL/HSIL/CIN)** treated with normal follow-up Pap: Generally eligible
- **⏸️ Currently undergoing treatment:** Temporary deferral until treatment complete + 6 months with normal Pap

#### Syphilis:
- **✅ Documented adequate treatment per CDC guidelines:** Eligible immediately after completion
- **❌ Untreated:** NOT eligible until treated

#### CMV:
- **❌ Ever tested positive OR diagnosed:** NOT eligible
- Lifelong infection that cannot be cured

### Sample Use

**Q: What happens to my sample (research)?**  
A: It's analyzed for stem cell quality and viability, maybe frozen for retesting, stored for up to 9 months, then properly disposed of. Not sold, not used commercially.

**Q: What happens to my sample (commercial)?**  
A: Cells are isolated, grown, and their beneficial secretions are collected. Then all cells are filtered out, leaving cell-free ingredient that's sold to cosmetic manufacturers.

**Q: Could my cells be used for cloning?**  
A: Nope! First, human cloning is illegal. Second, commercial products are totally cell-free - all cells are removed.

**Q: Could my DNA be studied?**  
A: **Research study:** possibly for research purposes, but only with de-identified samples. **Commercial program:** cells might be analyzed during processing but final product is cell-free.

**Q: Can I take back my donation after processing starts?**
